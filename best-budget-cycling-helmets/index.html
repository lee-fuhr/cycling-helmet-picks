A decent helmet is the only non-negotiable piece of cycling gear. You don’t need to mortgage your bike to stay safe, but picking the wrong budget helmet can mean poor fit, foggy vents, or a buckle that digs in. The good news: MIPS (Multi-directional Impact Protection System) has trickled down to very affordable models, and even entry-level helmets now offer features once reserved for the premium shelves. When you’re shopping on a budget, focus on fit first – a helmet that doesn’t sit level or can’t be snugged properly won’t protect as well as one that does. Next, look for ample ventilation (you’ll ride longer if your head stays cool) and a simple, secure retention system. We’ve combed through owner forums, professional reviews, and long-term feedback to pick three helmets that deliver real safety and comfort without breaking the bank.
